# 7 Insider Tips for Flawless Mascara Application

Do you need a few pointers for getting your mascara right? Applying mascara is an art that you wouldn't think would be hard, but it often is. I can't count the number of times I've ended up with mascara smeared under my eyes or accidentally poked myself with the wand. Fortunately, applying mascara effectively isn't complicated once you know the basics. These practical tips will help you achieve a cleaner, more voluminous look every time.

## 1. Find the Right Mascara

![Find the Right Mascara](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/y9/bp/gfs2z5vt_600x639.jpg)

Applying mascara is infinitely easier if you start with the right formula. Do you want to lengthen short lashes, or thicken thin ones? Perhaps you just want them to look darker and more defined. Assess what you want from your makeup routine, and pick a product designed for that specific purpose. If you are looking for reliable options, brands like [Maybelline](https://www.maybelline.com/eye-makeup/mascara) offer excellent varieties for every lash type.

## 2. Don’t Pump

![Don’t Pump](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fw/st/pxvdt7am_500x727.jpg)

When you pump the mascara wand up and down in the tube, you force air inside. This dries out the product and creates air pockets, leading to an uneven, clumpy application. Instead, makeup experts suggest gently twisting the wand in a circular motion before pulling it out of the tube. This simple habit keeps your mascara fresh for much longer.

## 3. Coat Both Sides

![Coat Both Sides](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/mm/w1/xqearp2m_600x900.jpg)

Most people only apply mascara to the underside of their lashes. It turns out that swiping it on both the front and back yields far better results. This technique creates a more defined, fuller look by fully coating each lash. It takes a little practice to avoid smudging your [eyeshadow](https://makeup.allwomenstalk.com/perfect-eyeshadow-looks/), but the dramatic improvement is worth the effort.

## 4. Curl First

![Curl First](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/i9/p0/sdapar19_600x901.jpg)

Some people apply mascara before using an eyelash curler, but this is a common mistake. Curling your lashes after applying mascara makes them brittle and prone to breakage. It also leaves your curler gunky and unhygienic. Always curl your bare lashes first, then follow up with mascara to lock the shape in place safely.

## 5. Wiggle the Wand

![Wiggle the Wand](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/lj/e2/j7pddtlm_600x763.jpg)

Instead of swiping from top to bottom in one swift motion, try wiggling the wand at your lash line before pulling it outward. This zigzag motion helps distribute the product evenly and separates the lashes beautifully. Paired with a good [eyeliner technique](https://makeup.allwomenstalk.com/eyeliner-tips-for-beginners/), this method prevents spiky, clumped-together lashes and gives you a soft, fluttery finish.

## 6. Use Warm Water

![Use Warm Water](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ui/ku/yl6grlwx_500x624.jpg)

If your mascara gets a little thick or difficult to apply, try soaking the sealed tube in a glass of warm water for a few minutes. The gentle heat softens the formula, making it glide on smoothly again. However, if the mascara is consistently clumpy and dried out, it is likely time to replace it altogether.

## 7. Throw It Away

![Throw It Away](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/bb/p5/hqzwf6cu_500x500.jpg)

You might not realize it, but eye makeup has a strict expiration date. The [FDA advises](https://www.fda.gov/cosmetics/cosmetic-products/eye-cosmetic-safety) replacing mascara every two to four months to prevent bacterial growth. Using expired mascara significantly increases your risk of developing eye infections. Protect your eyes by tossing old tubes, and never share mascara with anyone else.

Getting your lashes looking flawless can take some practice, but these techniques make a noticeable difference. Pay attention to how you apply the product, and you'll find what works best for your eye shape. What are your favorite tricks for a perfect mascara application?
