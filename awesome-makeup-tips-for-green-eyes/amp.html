11 Awesome Makeup Tips for Green Eyes ...

By Heather • 18 Comments

Makeup tips for green eyes are a game-changer, aren't they? Honestly, if I had a dollar for every time someone with mesmerizing green eyes asked me for makeup advice, I'd probably be sipping mimosas on a beach somewhere instead of writing intros. A quick glance in the mirror reassures me that the struggle—and joy—of making those irises pop is very real. As we sail through 2024, let's dive into some brilliant makeup tips that will not only make your green eyes sparkle but also turn more than a few heads.

One of the most exciting things about having green eyes is the versatility of eyeshadow palettes you get to play with. Seriously, the range is like being a kid in a candy store! From shimmering golds to deep purples, the world is your oyster. And speaking of oysters, did you know that the right eyeliner can make your eyes look as lustrous as pearls? We're talking about colors like bold blues and intense blacks. Yup, it's a thing, and it works.

Now, let's talk mascara. Oh, the drama a good coat of mascara can add! Think of it as the cherry on top of your makeup sundae. Going waterproof? Great! Opting for volumizing? Even better! And while we're at it, remember that eyeliner technique you were skeptical about? Trust me, put the cats back in cat-eye! The effect on green eyes is undeniably chic. And hey, if you mess it up, just call it a "smoky eye" and move on with your fabulous self.

You know, a highlighter can be your best friend or your worst enemy, like that roommate who knows all your secrets. When used correctly, it can make your eyes glow like you've just emerged from a high-end spa. But use too much and you'll look like a disco ball from the 80s—fun at parties, not at brunch. The secret lies in subtlety and knowing exactly where to swipe.

A little personal anecdote: I've been experimenting with different blush shades since the 90s. And guess what? I recently discovered that a soft peach shade does wonders for green eyes. Seriously, where was this tip when I was channeling my inner grunge queen?

Considering the buzz around makeup trends in 2024, incorporating just a few savvy tips from these sections will revolutionize your makeup game. So, buckle up and let’s embark on this fabulous journey together. Who knows? Your green-eyed glam may become the talk of the town!

P.S. Spoiler alert: We’ve got some other exciting tips lined up like perfecting the art of blending eyeshadow. Trust me, you won’t want to miss it.

1 Warm Bronze

Green eyes are all about the warm colors, the bronzes and golds and I gotta say, bronze eyeshadow is ideal for green eyes! This makeup tip for green eyes really works beautifully and will really make the green of your eyes pop right out! Trust me ladies, it's beautiful!

***

Bronze hues not only complement green eyes but also enhance their natural vibrancy. When you're choosing your eyeshadow, go for a shimmering bronze palette that reflects light and adds dimension to your eyes. A little bit of a copper tone mixed in with bronze can also work wonders, especially if your green eyes have hints of hazel. Don't be afraid to experiment with different intensities for a daytime look or a more dramatic evening effect. Pair your eyeshadow with a brown eyeliner to keep the warm theme going, and you'll see just how stunning your eyes can look.

2 Copper Tones

Just like bronze, this makeup tip for green eyes is all about the warmer tones. Copper tones work great for any and all green eyes because it really makes them pop and really makes them look beautiful. For my eyes, I always layer on a bit of shimmer to really let my eye color deepen!

***

When it comes to makeup tips for green eyes, copper tones are a great choice. Copper tones can help to make green eyes look brighter and more vibrant, and they can also help to deepen the eye color. Copper tones can be used in a variety of ways, such as with eyeshadow, eyeliner, and even mascara.

When applying copper tones to the eyes, it is important to use a light hand. Too much copper can be overwhelming and can make the eyes look too intense. Instead, start with a light layer of eyeshadow and then add more as needed. If you are using an eyeliner, use a thin line to create a subtle, yet noticeable, effect. For mascara, choose a brown or black shade, as this will help to enhance the copper tones.

In addition to using copper tones, it is also important to use complementary colors. For example, if you are wearing a copper eyeshadow, pair it with a light pink or peach blush and a light pink or peach lipstick. This will help to create a beautiful, balanced look.

3 Play with Purples

Purples and greens are two colors that go really well together! Ladies, if you are looking for the right makeup tips for green eyes, play with the purple eyeshadows and the violet eyeliners, see what works best for you!

***

If you have green eyes, playing with purple shades is a great way to bring out their natural beauty. Purple eyeshadows and violet eyeliners are especially flattering for green eyes. To make your eyes really stand out, try using a light purple eyeshadow on the inner corner of your eye and a darker purple shade on the outer corner. You can also use a light purple eyeliner on your lower lash line, and a deeper purple on your upper lash line. For a more dramatic look, use a shimmery purple eyeshadow to highlight the area beneath your brow bone. With a few simple tips, you can create a stunning eye look that will make your green eyes pop!

4 No Silver

One color that does not exactly work for green eyes is silver. Now, I am not talking about jewelry of course, but silver and gray eyeshadows do not exactly look all that great with green eyes. They conflict a little bit. Stick to warm colors and plums.

***

When it comes to makeup for green eyes, there are many different colors that you can use to bring out the natural beauty of your eyes. However, there is one color that you should avoid when it comes to green eyes: silver. Silver and gray eyeshadows can clash with green eyes, making them look dull and unappealing. Instead, you should opt for warm colors and plums.

When using makeup for green eyes, you should also think about the shape of your eyes. If you have round eyes, you should use eyeshadow to create a more almond-shaped look. You can do this by applying a light color to the inner corner of your eyes and a darker color to the outer corner. This will help to create more definition and make your eyes look bigger.

When it comes to eyeliner, it is important to choose the right color. For green eyes, you should opt for brown or black eyeliner. Avoid using blue or green eyeliner as this can make your eyes look too bright. You should also make sure to use a waterproof eyeliner so that it does not smudge or run.

5 Skip the Black Liner

Now, I wouldn't say that you have to skip the black liner all of the time, but this makeup tip for green eyes is all surrounding making the green pop. Plum and violet eyeliner is much better for green eyes and really will make the green in your eyes stand out that much more!

***

When it comes to makeup tips for green eyes, one of the most important ones is to skip the black liner. Black liner can be too harsh and overpowering for green eyes, so it’s best to opt for a softer color like plum or violet. These shades will really make the green in your eyes stand out and add a stunning contrast.

When it comes to eyeshadow, you want to stick with shades that will enhance the green in your eyes. Neutral shades like brown, gray, and taupe will help bring out the green without overpowering it. You can also try shades like gold or bronze, which will give your eyes a warm and inviting look.

Another great makeup tip for green eyes is to add a bit of white eyeliner in the inner corner of your eyes. This will help to open up the eyes and make them look bigger. You can also use a bit of white eyeliner along your waterline to make your eyes look brighter.

6 Darker Greens

While a lot of makeup tips for green eyes say you need to avoid all greens, I don't think that is true! If you have light green eyes, framing them in darker green or allowing some dark green eyeshadow is great! Remember, just go darker or lighter than your own green eyes color.

***

Using darker shades of green eyeshadow can create a striking contrast that makes your eye color pop. Consider shades like forest, emerald, or moss as they can complement your natural green beautifully. Blend them into the crease for depth, or apply along your lower lash line as a smoky accent. When selecting green tones, you want to ensure they are a few shades darker than your iris to avoid a monochromatic look that could wash out your eye color instead of enhancing it.

7 Peach Blush

Another makeup tip for green eyes is using peaches and cream blush. Whether it is cream or it is powder, I gotta say, peaches and cream blush really will make your eye color pop out!

***

Adding a peaches and cream blush to your cheeks creates a warm, youthful glow that emphasizes the natural vibrancy of green eyes. The key is in finding the right undertone—one that complements, rather than overshadows, your eye color. Look for blushes with a hint of gold shimmer to catch the light and play up the flecks of color in your iris. Lightly dust it on the apples of your cheeks for an everyday look, or build it up for a more dramatic evening effect. Just remember, blending is your friend to avoid harsh lines.

8 Red-Based Shadows

For your eye shadows in general, try to find colors that are red-based rather than blue-based. Red-based colors bring out the beautiful green in your eyes even more!

***

Shades like burgundy, maroon, and rust have the perfect undertones to complement your emerald peepers. When you're choosing red-based eyeshadows, make sure they have a warm tone, so they don't clash with your eye color. A subtle wash of these hues on the eyelid, especially when blended well into the crease, can add depth and warmth to your look. Don’t be afraid to experiment with different textures, such as matte for daytime sophistication or shimmer for a touch of evening glamour.

9 Pile on the Mascara

Green-eyed girls, don't be afraid to pile on the mascara! Green eyes are so rare and breathtaking that you should want them to be noticed! You should wear extra-lengthening mascara and attract attention to your eyes.

***

Green eyes are a beautiful rarity, and they deserve to be accentuated with the right makeup. Mascara is the perfect way to draw attention to green eyes and make them stand out. When applying mascara, opt for a lengthening formula that will emphasize the length of your lashes and make your eyes look bigger. Be sure to apply the mascara to the upper and lower lashes, and use a lash comb to separate the lashes, giving them a more natural look.

When it comes to eyeshadow, darker shades like black, brown, and navy blue are best for green eyes. These colors will bring out the green hue in your eyes, making them look brighter and more vibrant. For a more dramatic look, use a shimmery eyeshadow in the same colors. For a softer look, use light browns and beiges.

When it comes to eyeliner, dark brown or black are best for green eyes. Use a soft pencil eyeliner to line the upper and lower lash line. If you want a more dramatic look, use a liquid eyeliner for the upper lash line. Finish off your look with a few coats of volumizing mascara and you’ll be ready to show off your beautiful green eyes.

10 Brown Mascara

Brown mascara can look subtly beautiful on women with green eyes! It's simple, yet makes a considerable difference in your appearance!

***

Brown mascara is an excellent choice for women with green eyes as it can bring out the color and emphasize the eyes in a subtle way. For the best results, opt for a brown mascara with golden undertones as this will add a hint of warmth to the eyes and enhance the natural color. It is important to choose the right shade of brown mascara to match your eye color. If you have light green eyes, a light brown mascara is ideal. For darker green eyes, try a darker brown mascara with a hint of black.

When applying brown mascara, start at the base of the lashes and wiggle the wand back and forth as you move up. This will help to separate the lashes and give them a natural, full look. Once you have finished applying the mascara to the top lashes, you can go back and apply a second coat to the bottom lashes. For a more dramatic look, use a waterproof brown mascara which will help to keep the color in place all day.

11 Pinks and Plums

Finally ladies, the last makeup tips for green eyes is to use the pinks and plums! Whether it is in your eyeliner or in your eyeshadow, incorporate the colors somehow and it'll really make your eye color pop!

So ladies, there are all of my makeup tips for green eyes, what tips do you have? Have you ever used any of these makeup tips for green eyes? Give 'em a try!
