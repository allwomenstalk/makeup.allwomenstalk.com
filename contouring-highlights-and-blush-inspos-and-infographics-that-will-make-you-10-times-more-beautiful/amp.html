Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between contouring and highlighting?
Contouring uses darker shades to define and slim your face, while highlighting uses lighter shades to bring forward features you want to emphasize.
How do I choose the right contour shade for my skin?
Pick a contour shade that is 1-2 tones darker than your natural skin tone and avoid anything too warm or orange.
Where should I apply contour on my face?
Apply contour under your cheekbones, along the jawline, sides of the nose, and your forehead edges for a sculpted look.
What’s the best way to blend contour and highlight?
Use a damp beauty sponge or a fluffy brush in gentle circular motions for a seamless, natural finish.
How do I pick the perfect blush color for my skin tone?
Choose a blush shade that complements your undertones: rosy for cool tones, peach or coral for warm tones, and soft plum for deeper skin.
Can I use contour and highlight with any makeup style?
Yes! Both can be adapted to subtle daytime looks or dramatic evening makeup depending on the intensity and placement.
Should I use cream or powder contour products?
Cream contours are great for dry skin and a dewy finish, while powders work well for oily skin and longer wear.
How do highlights enhance my facial features?
Highlights catch light on the high points like cheekbones and brow bones, creating a lifted and radiant effect.
Is blush necessary if I’m using contour and highlight?
Blush adds a healthy flush and brings balance to the face, so it’s a great step to complete your makeup.
What tools are best for applying blush?
Use a soft, angled brush for powder blush or your fingers for cream blush for precise and natural application.
