2 How to Contour Your Body with Self-Tanner

Source: How to Contour Your Body

***

To achieve a sculpted look with self-tanner, start by exfoliating to create a smooth canvas. Choose a tanner that's one or two shades darker than your natural skin tone for a subtle contour. Apply it to areas you want to define, such as beneath your cheekbones, along the sides of your nose, and under the jawline. Use a blending brush to ensure seamless transitions. Then, enhance the contrast by applying a lighter shade on areas you want to highlight, like the center of your legs to create an illusion of slimmer limbs. Always blend well for a natural finish. Remember, practice makes perfect!

3 How to Contour for Different Face Shapes

Source: 10 Pinterest infographics that'll turn

***

Understanding your face shape is key to perfect contouring. For oval faces, darken the areas you'd like to slim, typically under the cheekbones and along the hairline. Round faces benefit from shadowing the sides of the face to create angles and highlighting the center of the forehead and chin to elongate. Those with a square face should soften their jawline and forehead by contouring these areas, while emphasizing the cheekbones. Heart-shaped faces require contour under the cheeks and sides of the forehead to balance the face, and a touch of highlighter on the chin can add symmetry.

Understanding your face shape is a cornerstone for perfecting your contouring and highlighting routine. 10 10 Commandments of Blush

Source: beauty

***

To ensure your blush remains your beauty ally, remember to pick a color that complements your skin tone—think peaches and pinks for a fresh, natural look on lighter skin, and rich berries and warm tans for deeper tones. Never forget to tap off excess powder from your brush to avoid overly intense color and consider the natural fall of light on your face to locate the ideal placement. To seamlessly integrate blush with your contour and highlight, use a fluffy brush in soft, circular motions, building intensity gradually for that perfect, sun-kissed flush that looks like it's truly your own.

13 Correct Nose Contour

Source: makeup

***

To achieve a slender and more defined nose, start by selecting a contour shade slightly darker than your skin tone. Using a small, angled brush, draw a line along both sides of your nose, connecting to the brows for a natural look. Remember to blend thoroughly for a seamless finish, avoiding harsh lines. Additionally, a touch of highlighter down the center of the nose can accentuate straightness and narrowness. Practice makes perfect, so don't be discouraged if it takes several attempts to get it just right.

16 How to Contour Your Face

Source: Makeup Tutorial: How to Contour

***

Contouring is an essential skill that sculpts and defines your facial features. Start by selecting a matte bronzer or contour powder a few shades darker than your natural skin tone. With a contour brush or angled brush, apply along the hollows of your cheeks, the sides of your nose, and around your hairline on your forehead. The key is blending; make sure there are no harsh lines. For additional definition, highlight above the cheekbones, on the bridge of your nose, and the center of your forehead. This contrast illuminates and elevates your features for that coveted three-dimensional look.
