# How to Apply Makeup on Sensitive Eyes without Irritating Them ...

You shouldn’t have to avoid makeup because you have sensitive eyes. You know the story. You apply makeup and your eyes start to stream and itch or you apply it successfully but then after a short time it’s weepy central. With some simple steps however, you can enjoy cosmetics as much as the next girl.

## 1. Take an Antihistamine

![Take an Antihistamine](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/zi/qs/iebt6ay3571144926614e442381259_600x600.jpg)

One good way to avoid irritating sensitive eyes when putting on eye makeup is to take an antihistamine in preparation for applying it. It might sound extreme, but antihistamines do wonder for people with allergies. It makes sense that the effects of the medication might be able to make a difference for somebody who is going to willingly irritate their sensitive skin. Many people who do this say that it significantly reduces redness and tearfulness that can occur.

## 2. Use Primer

![Use Primer](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/id/hz/jbpqiy4l571144bfb7d81199879475_600x600.jpg)

Another great tip is to use a primer before applying your makeup. It might not work for the most sensitive eyes, but as a form of protection for slight irritation, the layer of primer can work as a thin barrier between your skin and the makeup that you are putting on. Having that thin layer of primer can be the difference between getting horrible red eyes and your makeup staying on and not bothering you at all.

## 3. Think about Your Hormones

![Think about Your Hormones](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/nk/mu/ohj4xi0l571144d2c1538985433187_600x600.jpg)

It is a fact that our hormones are closely linked with our immune system, so as our hormones fluctuate over the course of a month, so too does our immune system. As a result of this linked relationship, your skin might be more prone to being irritated at certain times of the month when your hormones are at their most erratic. Listen to your body, and perhaps avoid putting on too much makeup on the days that you feel at your most hormonal.

## 4. Don’t Start Mascara at the Root

![Don’t Start Mascara at the Root](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/cp/tp/bwib1mm1571144f7f2653958673426_600x600.jpg)

If you have dark eyelashes this is something that you can definitely get away with. Instead of starting from the root and curling up wards, miss out the beginning of the root altogether. This way, no makeup with actually run the risk of coming in to contact with your sensitive skin, and you will still be able to see the effects of the mascara because of the volume that will have been created by applying to the middle and the tips of the lashes.

## 5. Natural Products

![Natural Products](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/c8/g5/qi4ayf1x57114547743b6678069610_600x600.jpg)

They might be a touch more expensive, but if sensitive skin is something that you really suffer from, then you might want to consider exploring the world of all natural makeup products. They used to be niche products but now you can pretty much find natural products in all good stores. They are kinder to your skin.

## 6. Watch out for Carmine

![Watch out for Carmine](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/sb/i4/l081h9ro5711455e2127d239928555_600x600.jpg)

When buying products, always look out for the ingredients list to see if they contain something called carmine. It is often very common in products that are varying shades of red and purple but it can be found in shadows and mascara. Carminic acid is something that can very easily irritate and bother women who have notoriously sensitive skin. In more serious cases, the presence of carminic acid has also been known to bring out people in hives.

## 7. Throw Away Old Products

![Throw Away Old Products](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/tp/gu/unbs21ge57114588bf7d6973799872_600x600.jpg)

If you are still using a mascara that you have had for many months, then it would be a good idea to throw it away and treat yourself to a brand new one. The reason for this is that old makeup products, especially mascara, are prone to picking up a lot of bacteria, and this is not at all ideal for somebody who has ultra-sensitive eyes to begin with. The fresher the makeup, the less likely you are to have a reaction.

## 8. Avoid Powder Shadows

![Avoid Powder Shadows](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/r6/fk/dsodozmy571145cfae5ef159800178_600x600.jpg)

Even the tiniest flecks of powder can irritate sensitive eyes. Cream shadows are a much better choice. As are cream blushes. As you flick the blush brush, minute drops of blush powder can irritate just like shadow. Also something to think about it you use powder over foundation. Any powder cosmetic is best avoided tbh.

## 9. Keep Brushes and Applicators Clean

![Keep Brushes and Applicators Clean](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ti/qq/n04ojp6b571145ec1c55c269031749_600x600.jpg)

Just how old products attract and contain bacteria, so do your makeup brushes. Clean them carefully and regularly. Be sure to use something that won’t cause eye irritation. A baby wash will work.

Remember also to be gentle. Don’t aggravate sensitivity or irritation by being too rough when you apply eye makeup. Change your products regularly and always take your makeup off properly, every day.
