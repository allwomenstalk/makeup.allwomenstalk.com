11 Wicked Amazing Eyeliner Tips for Almond Eyes ...

Follow Contour of Your Eyelids • Know Your Eye Size • Ideal for the Winged Eyeliner • Thicker Eyeliner for Night Time • Thinner Eyeliner for Day Time • More ...

By Heather

Wicked amazing eyeliner tips have always been my secret weapon to making my almond eyes pop! As someone who has spent countless hours perfecting the art of eyeliner (and let's be real, wasting an equal amount of time on unsuccessful attempts), I’ve got a treasure trove of tricks to share with you all in this hilariously comprehensive guide. If your goal in 2025 is to master the mysterious and ancient art of eyelid enchantment, then look no further!

Now, let me tell you, almond eyes are like a built-in advantage in the world of makeup. Seriously, it’s like being born with a natural contour! The versatile shape lends itself beautifully to all kinds of looks-from everyday chic to show-stopping glam. But that doesn’t mean we’re immune to the dreaded "panda eyes" or uneven wings. So, buckle up for a fun ride filled with eyeliner wisdom.

First off, understanding your eye shape is essential, and I've nailed down some killer cat-eye tricks that will make your eyes look like they’re straight out of a Hollywood classic. I've even managed to get that perfect eyeliner flick while riding the Metro (not ideal, but sometimes you gotta do what you gotta do). And hey, isn’t the beauty of eyeliner in its ability to transform you from 'I just woke up' to 'I woke up like this'?

Next up, let’s talk tools of the trade. Oh, the journey through various eyeliner types. From liquid liners that could double as calligraphy pens to gel liners smooth as butter, making the right choice can make or break your eyeliner game. The universe seriously needs to give a medal to whoever invented the felt-tip pen. And if you’re retro like me, you might even have a secret love affair with pencil liners, especially when paired with a lift-up-my-lashes kit to give your vintage look that irresistible wide-eyed finish.

One of the cornerstone tips involves pinpointing the ideal eyeliner angle for enhancing your eye shape. Ah yes, the delicate balance of extending that line just right! Not too steep or you'll look perpetually surprised, and not too flat else you'll lose the drama. Oh, the drama! Did I mention it's all about finding that sweet spot?

We’ll also dive deep into water-proofing techniques, because let’s face it-as much as we love a good laugh or cry, racoon eyes are nobody’s friend. 2024 boasts better makeup options than ever, so say goodbye to streaky messes and hello to smudge-free days!

So grab your favorite liner, maybe a cup of coffee (or a glass of wine-no judgment here), and embark on this fun journey to slay your eyeliner game. We're in this together, fabulous almond-eyed friends!

1. Follow Contour of Your Eyelids

Eyeliner tips for almond eyes almost always start out the same – know your eye shape, think about dark liner. However, I think that following the contour of your eyelids is honestly the most important eyeliner tip when you are dealing with almond eyes. You want them to stand out, and following along from end to end will do that for you!

2. Know Your Eye Size

Remember when I mentioned your eye size? Well, you do want to know exactly how big your eyes are. This will determine how much eyeliner you need and what you are going to use. If you have smaller eyes, you want to go with thinner lines; wider eyes and bigger eyes, you want something a little thicker!

3. Ideal for the Winged Eyeliner

Did you know that almond shaped eyes are ideal for winged eyeliner? If you've never done winged eyeliner and you have almond shaped eyes, give it a try! Just a flick of your wrist upward at the end of your eye can really bring your eyes together and make them stand out! I actually go a little crazy with the winged liner for my eyes and draw it out a little bit more toward my eyebrow. It's chic and totally different!

4. Thicker Eyeliner for Night Time

With almond shaped eyes, you can go with a little thicker line – again, depending on how big or small your eyes are. Your individual shape and size will determine exactly how thick the liner should be. But remember, night time is the time to play, so really play up your eyes and make them stand out!

5. Thinner Eyeliner for Day Time

This is the opposite to #4, right? Thinner eyeliner can still make your eyes look beautiful and stand out, but it'll also be appropriate for work and even for a job interview. Thinner eyeliner doesn't stand out as much, but it'll give you that oomph that us girls look for!

6. Use the Lash Line as Your Guide

When you have almond eyes, you might not have the longest lashes in the world – then again, you might have super long eyelashes! Always use your lash line as your guide for your eyeliner. That way, you can make sure that you're really putting on your best look.

7. No Eyeliner along the Bottom Lid

There should be no eyeliner along your bottom lid. For me, I usually just line my eyes on my waterline and it really makes them pop. Try this trick and see just how awesome your eyes look! This trick can actually work for anyone that is trying not to put liner along their bottom lid.

8. Use White Liner for a Doe-eyed Look

Although black eyeliner is the more traditional pick, white eyeliner is just as good. In fact, it's perfect for making your eyes look bigger and brighter. I always add a little bit on the inner corner of my eyes because it makes them really stand out!

9. Smudge a Bit

Remember, not all lines have to precise. A little smudging never hurt nobody! If you line the bottom of your eyes, trace your lash line and then smudge it toward the outer corners. It'll make your eyes look wider and larger!

10. Nude Colors

You don't always have to use liner to define your eyes. If you want a subtle look that widens your eyes, try using a nude color like the Chromagraphic Pencil from maccosmetics.com (in NC15/NW20).

11. Leave Some of Your Eye Naked

Have you heard of the "dropped eyeliner" trick? It's when you draw a full line on top and apply liner to only half your bottom lid (starting from the outer corner in) You’ll be amazed at just how much it can alter your style!

So all you almond-shaped eyed girls, what other tips do you have when it comes to eyeliner? Did I get 'em all? Let us know in the comments, we love to hear from you!

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best eyeliner style for almond eyes?
Winged eyeliner or cat eyes enhance the natural shape of almond eyes beautifully.
Should I use pencil, gel, or liquid eyeliner on almond eyes?
Liquid eyeliner works great for precise lines, but gel gives more control for softer looks.
How can I make my eyeliner last all day?
Use a primer on your eyelids before applying eyeliner to prevent smudging and fading.
Can I tightline my almond eyes?
Yes, tightlining makes your lashes look fuller and adds subtle definition without overpowering your almond shape.
What's a quick way to fix uneven eyeliner?
Use a cotton swab dipped in makeup remover to gently clean up and even out the lines.
How do I avoid eyeliner smudging on oily eyelids?
Set your eyeliner with a matching eyeshadow or translucent powder to keep it in place.
Is it better to line just the upper or both lash lines?
Lining the upper lash line opens eyes wide, but adding a subtle line on the lower lash can enhance almond eyes further.
Can colored eyeliner work well on almond eyes?
Yes! Jewel tones and metallic eyeliners really pop and complement almond eyes beautifully.
How can I create a smoky eyeliner look for almond eyes?
Apply a pencil liner and smudge gently with a brush for a soft gradient that flatters almond shapes.
What's an easy eyeliner hack for beginners with almond eyes?
Start with small dashes along the lash line instead of one continuous line for better control.
